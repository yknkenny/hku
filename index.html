Computer Vision for On-street Tree Mapping

Using GSV images collected in Feb 2022 to map update on-street trees/plants along Tiu Keng Leng, Hong Kong

Advantages of Computer Vision for Mapping

Not every mapping features are mapped and opened up by the government. Trees and plants in Hong Kong are the example. Mapping from computer vision provides an alternative way of data acquisition to understand our Earth.

Free and Convenient

GSV services are free of use in Google Map. Google updated panoramas every 1 to 2 years. The panoramas in Tiu Keng Leng () captured in Feb 2022 were released on Google Map in early Mar 2022.

Abundant GSV Data for Analytics

Every GSV view covers a 360-panoramic image and the geographic positions of captured features stored in respective 'depth map' and attibutes.

Mature and Low-cost AI Technologies

Deep learning technologies for computer vision, especially for recognition of trees and plants are mature. The computational costs are relatively low.
